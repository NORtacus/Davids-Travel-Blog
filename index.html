Christmas on Color Line

The international ferry routes from Norway to either Denmark or Germany tend to be very cheap. This is because they make good money from travelers spending money on board the ferry at the many restaurants, bars, activities on board or spending money in the taxfre shops. It is known that the prices of alcoholic products in Norway are quite high, so you can make many bargains in the duty-free shops.

Every November or December, I and my family take a trip with color line to Kiel in Germany. Since I am a member of the color line club, I often get good offers and the journey between Oslo and Kiel cost only NOK 398 per person when there are 2 of us sharing a cabin. It's very cheap for a 3 day mini cruise!

26.Okt.2023 - Tags: cruise, Finland

One day in Kiel
26.Okt.2023 - Tags: Kiel, Germany

The color line ferry from oslo is in port almost in the middle of kiel center from 10.00 - 14.00 so there is enough time to both eat and shop a bit with german prices that are quite a bit lower than the norwegian ones. It only takes 10 minutes to walk from the ferry into the center where there is a good selection of shops.

Kiel is a fairly large city with approximately 250,000 inhabitants in the very north of Germany. The city's location facing the sea makes it an important port city with, among other things, the German Navy's Baltic fleet and several vertfs. From Kiel you can also take the ferry to Sweden and Lithuania plus several cruise ships dock in the city.

Christmas on DFDS
26.Okt.2023 - Tags: cruiseferry, Denmark

Another ferry company that sails from Norway internationally is DFDS on the route Oslo - Copenhagen. They have older and smaller ferries than Color Line's route Oslo - Kiel, but in return, the price for the tickets is cheaper and the destination Copenhagen is more exciting than Kiel.

I had not planned to travel with a mini-cruise with DFDS, but received a very good offer by email. They had a campaign to buy a mini cruise for NOK 100, NOK 50 was donated to an organization that works to clean the sea of plastic waste. That is to say, in reality the ticket for the mini-cruise to/from Copenhagen cost including own cabin only NOK 50!

One day in Copenhagen
26.Okt.2023 - Tags: Copenhagen, Denmark

I like Copenhagen. The capital of Denmark, which is an old city with a lot of history and a lot of great architecture! Copenhagen has approximately 660,000 inhabitants and is quite large with many opportunities as a tourist. The only downside is that it is more expensive in Copenhagen than elsewhere in Norway, and this is partly due to the high Danish krone exchange rate.

The DFDS Danish ferry docks in Nordhavn, which is about 15 minutes by public transport to the center of Copenhagen. Then I usually take a train from Nordhavn station, which is a short walk from the ferry dock. There are also buses that go directly from the ferry, but they run less often and take longer. A ticket within the city center cost DKK 24.

Trip to Gdansk, Poland
26.Okt.2023 - Tags: Gdansk, Poland

From Norway we have very cheap flights with Wizzair or Ryanair down to Poland. they can offer cheaper tickets due to, among other things, Polish employees on the flights and they are a low cos carrier which means they charge for everything extra on the flight. I only paid NOK 109 for the flight from Trondheim to Gdansk, but they charge a full NOK 550 for checked baggage, so here you have to travel with a small rucksack!

Gdansk offers a lot. The biggest reason why many Norwegians travel there is because Poland is very cheap! All food, tobacco and alcohol products are among the cheapest in Europe. Not only that, Poland has many fine cities with a lot of history and great architecture, this can also be said about Gdansk.

Christmas tram in Trondheim
26.Okt.2023 - Tags: public transport, Trondheim, Norway

The tram in Trondheim is the world's northernmost tram, after they shut down the tram line in Arkhangelsk, Russia. It is only 8.8 km long, starting in the middle of the city up to Lian in Bymarka, which is a popular hiking area in Trondheim. Work is underway to extend the tram line east to Lade and south to Saupstad. The track width is 1000 mm, which is unusual, as most tram networks have a track width of 1435 mm. Boreal Transport operates the tram in Trondheim on behalf of AtB, which is owned by Trøndelag County Municipality

Every December, one of the trams is decorated with Christmas lights outside and Christmas decorations inside. Sometimes the tram driver is also dressed in a Santa suit. We took a trip with the Christmas tram!
